Why I like CS

Introduction to Computer Science was radically different than any class I’d ever taken in high school. I wasn’t asked to memorize anything. I wasn’t expected to passively absorb what my teacher was telling me. In fact I was encouraged to test what my teacher spoke about in lecture, and I learned by watching my programs fail, and tinkering with them to get them to work.

I don’t think humans are optimized for passive learning. We seem to be better at active learning. I think this is why figuring stuff out is so intensely satisfying. In CS classes I am constantly trying to figure stuff out. Accepting that I’ve learned everything there is for me to learn is a nightmare I want to avoid at all costs. I want to tinker with things for the rest of my life. This is why I’m pursuing a CS degree.
